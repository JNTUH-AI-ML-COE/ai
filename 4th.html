Full Stack Modern AI: From Experiments to Production

Graduate Level · Two-Semester Sequence · 2025

Description Outcomes Prerequisites Format Weekly Syllabus References

Course Overview

This two-semester graduate sequence teaches students to design, build, and deploy AI systems end-to-end, evolving machine learning experiments into robust, production-ready applications. The course emphasizes full-stack AI engineering: ML pipelines, scalable model deployment, monitoring, and ethical operations. Cutting-edge topics include MLOps, LLMOps, AgentOps, AI SecOps, large language models, autonomous agents, federated learning, and secure & responsible AI.

Learning Objectives

  • Architect and automate ML pipelines for continuous data ingest, training, testing, and deployment, with built-in versioning and reproducibility.
  • Deploy and scale models as reliable services on cloud and edge infrastructure, meeting targets for latency, throughput, and uptime.
  • Monitor, evaluate, and maintain ML systems in production, detecting data drift and degrading model performance – trigger retraining or rollbacks.
  • Ensure trustworthiness and compliance of AI solutions by design – addressing privacy, security, fairness, and ethics throughout the ML lifecycle.
  • Leverage advanced models and tools: LLMs, agent frameworks, federated learning, and more.
  • Work across tech stacks: build with TensorFlow, PyTorch, Databricks, MLflow, Kubeflow, GCP/AWS, and compare industry platforms.
  • Lead full-stack ML projects from definition to deployment and monitoring, including a two-semester capstone application project.

Prerequisites

  • Graduate-level background in machine learning or AI (introductory ML course or equivalent experience).
  • Strong Python programming skills.
  • Foundational knowledge of data science, cloud, and software engineering (git, notebooks, APIs).
  • Interest in engineering robust and ethical real-world AI systems.

Course Format & Assessment

  • Weekly lecture/seminar and discussion of AI systems, engineering, research, and demos.
  • Programming assignments and mini-projects: hands-on ML pipelines, deployments, MLOps tools.
  • Midterm quiz and presentation on advanced industry practices.
  • Team-based capstone project: design, build, and demo a fully functional production AI system.
  • Grading: 50% project, 30% assignments, 10% presentation, 10% participation.

Weekly Syllabus

Semester 1: Foundations of AI Engineering in Production

Semester 1 focuses on MLOps fundamentals and core skills for taking an ML model from development to initial production deployment. Students progress from basic pipelines to deploying a simple ML service with monitoring, while addressing data management and responsible AI practices.

  • Week 1: Introduction to MLOps and Full-Stack AI
    Overview of the machine learning development lifecycle vs. traditional software.
    Why ML systems fail when transitioning from lab to production; the need for MLOps to bridge this gap.
    Roles in an AI project (data scientist, ML engineer, DevOps) and course logistics.
  • Week 2: Machine Learning Pipeline & Process
    Detailed walkthrough of an end-to-end ML pipeline (from data acquisition to model serving). Version control for code and data, reproducibility, collaboration tools (Git, notebooks, project structure). Operational excellence: tracking and automating each stage (data, model, code).
  • Week 3: Data Engineering for ML
    Data governance and production preparation. Building data pipelines (ETL/ELT) and feature engineering workflows. Data versioning, feature stores, data quality, bias handling, privacy considerations.
  • Week 4: Experiment Tracking and Model Development
    Reproducible experimentation with tracking tools (MLflow/Weights & Biases). Best practices for modular code, pipeline config, team development. Reliable training, debugging, model understanding.
  • Week 5: Model Deployment I – Packaging and Serving
    Containerization with Docker. Creating RESTful API endpoints or cloud functions for predictions. Batch vs real-time serving. Local/cloud deployment practice.
  • Week 6: Model Deployment II – CI/CD and Automation
    Applying DevOps to ML: automated workflows for integration, testing, deployment. Infrastructure-as-Code basics, continuous training/delivery pipelines. Automate retraining with tools (GitHub Actions, Jenkins).
  • Week 7: AI Application Integration (APIs & User Interface)
    Expose ML models to end-users via APIs, front-end/mobile integration. User experience, communicating uncertainty/errors. Logging interactions for improvement. Mini-project: minimal demo app.
  • Week 8: Monitoring and Observability
    Post-deployment monitoring of ML systems. Telemetry: response latency, throughput, resource usage, prediction metrics. Drift detection, dashboards/alerts (Prometheus, Grafana), logging/monitoring agents.
  • Week 9: Testing and Quality Assurance for ML Systems
    End-to-end testing beyond offline accuracy. Unit/integration tests, A/B and canary releases, shadow deployments, reliability evaluation. Practice writing tests and updating models.
  • Week 10: Responsible AI – Ethics, Fairness, and Safety
    Bias detection/mitigation, fairness, explainability, regulatory compliance. Adversarial robustness, privacy techniques, engineer’s responsibility for ethics/safety.
  • Week 11: Industry Tools & MLOps Platforms
    Survey and comparison of Databricks, SageMaker, Vertex AI, Kubeflow, MLflow, etc. Criteria for tool/platform selection, integration, guest industry case studies.
  • Week 12: Project Phase I – Building a Production ML Pipeline
    Teams design/implement a pipeline for a sample problem, train/deploy as a monitored microservice. Emphasis on code quality, automation, documentation. Demo in-class as milestone for Semester 2.

Semester 2: Advanced Topics in AI Production and Operations

Semester 2 builds on the fundamentals to tackle advanced and emerging areas in deploying AI at scale. Cutting-edge practices for LLMs, AI agents, federated learning, security, and production readiness—plus a major capstone project.

  • Week 1: From Models to Foundation Models
    Transition from task-specific models to large pre-trained models (GPT-4, Vision Transformers). Compare architecture/deployment for traditional ML vs. large foundation models. Discuss API usage, fine-tuning needs.
  • Week 2: LLMOps – Building with Large Language Models
    Operationalizing LLMs, prompt engineering, RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation), pipelines and agents (LangChain/LlamaIndex). Hands-on: build LLM-powered feature with API or open-source model.
  • Week 3: Fine-Tuning and Optimizing Models
    Adapt/fine-tune large models (PEFT, LoRA) for tasks; optimize for inference with distillation, quantization, compression. Deploy and measure performance improvement.
  • Week 4: AI Agents and AgentOps
    Architect agentic AI (planning, tool use, memory, frameworks: AutoGPT, BabyAGI). Explore AgentOps for reliability, non-determinism, longevity. Instrument/design simple agent with observability.
  • Week 5: Agent Observability and Evaluation
    Monitor/evaluate autonomous agents with logging, step-wise replaying. Introduce new metrics for autonomy, goal completion, correctness, analytics for agent performance.
  • Week 6: Security & AI SecOps
    Secure ML against adversarial/malicious threats. Defenses: robust training, sanitization, filters. Hardening endpoints, detecting attacks via monitoring. Case studies and building guardrails.
  • Week 7: Privacy-Preserving ML and Federated Learning
    Federated learning, trusted/private compute (TEE, homomorphic encryption), differential privacy, regulatory compliance. Simulation/project: federated learning, privacy/noise trade-offs.
  • Week 8: Scaling AI Systems and Infrastructure
    Architecting reliable large-scale AI: data lakes, streaming/batch, real-time pipelines, Kubernetes, GPU clusters. Cost optimization, serverless inference, hands-on orchestration and load testing.
  • Week 9: Continuous Learning and Model Evolution
    Ongoing improvement: feedback, periodic re-training, online learning, safe rollbacks/deployment, AutoML. Implement and evaluate a workflow for self-updating AI.
  • Week 10: ML Platform Engineering & Case Studies
    Cohesive platform engineering: feature stores, registries, CI/CD, workflow automation. Industry case studies (Uber Michelangelo, Meta FBLearner, Netflix MLOps).
  • Week 11: AI Governance and Policy
    Documentation/audits, model cards, bias monitoring, regulatory standards, risk management, future AI oversight.
  • Week 12: Capstone Project Demo and Wrap-up
    Teams present end-to-end applications from ingestion/training to deployment, monitoring, and maintenance. Projects are evaluated on accuracy, readiness, scalability, and responsibility. Course wrap-up: key takeaways, trends, and future learning paths.
